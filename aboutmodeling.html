About XEM Modeling

Invented by our start-up team consisting of an experienced financial engineer from NYU (New York University) & certified financial risk manager (FRM), experience stock traders, software & big data engineer, Xide Empirical Model (“XEM”) is an empirical modeling based on extensive experiments & observations, and model performance measures & verifications. It is primarily designed to provide buy/sell advices at real-time setting during trading sessions (updated per minute), for tactical trading, instead of strategic investments.

Technically, XEM is built on our observations with the main two findings

A stock price is not a real random variable, as it is typically considered as a random variable in the financial world. A stock price is a set of discrete numbers, but with a nature of continuity in price movements in a given time period

A stock price has a certain memory in term of time period. Surprisingly, a stock price has quite short memory in matters of days, instead of months or year. A stock with higher price volatility has a shorter memory period, while a stock with lower volatility has a relatively longer memory period. XEM model is dynamically analyzing an individual stock price volatility to determine its memory time period, based on which the model further applies data analysis tool to determine its buy or sell points.
